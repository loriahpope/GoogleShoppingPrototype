Google Shopping Concept - Desktop

Category

Interaction Design

Inspiration

While Google has come to be known as a leader in user experience design, many of its own applications have yet to be upgraded. I wanted to bring Google Shopping into the Material Design world, while upgrading the experience a bit to highlight the products Google is becoming known for.

Heuristic Review

Home Page


The home page does not provide a focus or brand identity to the site. Google is becoming a household electronics name (Pixel, Home, Chromebook), so opportunity is lost to highlight this. There is no easy way for the user to browse across categories, so they must rely on searching or scrolling through the entire page. The page needs organization.

Search Results/View Item Page


The Search Results/View Item Page allows the user to view items in either a grid or list view. A category can be selected when the user clicks on a featured item from the home page. A variety of filter categories are available in the left-hand pane.


In either view, the user can click on the item to view an expanded detail view. This allows the user to view, along with the title, seller, rating, and price information, a detailed description as well. From this view, the user can 'shop', by clicking on a button to proceed to the seller's site.

Problem Definition

After using the Google Shopping site, I understand that this is not Google's main business. The entire site serves as a way to aggregate deals from other sites and display them on the Google Shopping site. A missed opportunity for this is highlighting the products Google has been producing, including Google Chromebooks, Google Pixel Phones, Google Chromecast, and Google Home.
